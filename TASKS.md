LLM playlist architecture Phase C landed on 2026-04-23: recovery is now an explicit relaxation ladder instead of an emergent fallback. Hub playlist generation progressively enables pools in this order: `exact-path` (`core`) → `adjacent-path` → `same-root` → `acoustic-similarity` → `mood-bridge` → `discovery-backfill`, stopping at the first level that yields enough distinct songs and artists for the requested playlist length. The highest relaxation level reached is now logged per playlist and also governs downstream behavior: genre anchoring is kept through `same-root` but dropped for acoustic-and-beyond recovery, and adaptive soft-veto recovery only activates once the playlist has crossed into non-genre recovery levels. Verified with `npx tsc --noEmit`.

Each playlist now emits a computed diversity score plus supporting diagnostics for artist count, album spread, genre-root spread, pool spread, acoustic-cluster spread, and mean pairwise acoustic distance. LLM playlist tuning follow-up 2 landed on 2026-04-23 after new diagnostics exposed veto-blind viability and multi-root anchor collapse. LLM playlist tuning follow-up 4 landed on 2026-04-23 after diagnostics showed a final rerank-stage collapse: some concepts still dropped even though the filtered candidate pool remained healthy.
